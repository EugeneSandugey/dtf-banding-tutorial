1. What is DTF Printer Banding?

Banding in DTF (Direct-to-Film) printing refers to the appearance of visible horizontal lines or streaks on printed images that appear in the direction of printhead carriage movement. Unlike ink smearing across the film, banding appears as inconsistent ink coverage, creating visible lines or streaks that ruin print quality.

Key Characteristics of DTF Banding:

  • Horizontal Lines: Most common type, running left to right
  • Inconsistent Coverage: Uneven ink deposition rather than smearing
  • Pattern Repetition: Often occurs in regular intervals
  • Color-Specific: May affect certain colors more than others

Horizontal Banding Visual Description:

Banding appears as visible horizontal lines running across your prints:

  • Appearance: Lines run left-to-right in the printhead movement direction
  • Pattern: May be evenly spaced or irregular
  • Severity: Can range from subtle to very obvious
  • Most visible: On solid color areas and gradients

For visual examples, see Procolored's banding examples or EazyDTF's banding guide

Important Note: Banding differs from printhead strikes or nozzle clogs. While clogs create missing dots or lines, banding shows as uneven ink density across the entire print area.

2. Types of DTF Banding Issues

2.1 Nozzle Check Patterns - Good vs Bad

Critical for Audley 2W+2C Configuration:

With 2 white heads and 2 color heads, you need to monitor all 4 printheads carefully. White heads are especially prone to clogging due to titanium dioxide settling.

Understanding Nozzle Check Patterns:

Learn to read nozzle check patterns to diagnose issues quickly:

Blue Channel Issues:
  • Missing segments in blue lines
  • Wavy or deflected patterns
  • Common after head cleaning
  • May need deep cleaning
Horizontal Banding Pattern:
  • Regular gaps in test lines
  • Indicates clogged nozzles
  • Shows as stripes in prints
  • Requires immediate cleaning
Color Dropouts:
  • Entire color missing
  • Partial channel failure
  • Check dampers first
  • May need ink prime

Resources: See real nozzle check examples at:

  • T-Shirt Forums - Blue Channel Issues (with images)
  • DIY DTF Forums - Nozzle Check Analysis
  • Direct Color Systems - Nozzle Test Guide (PDF)
  • Good Pattern: Complete, unbroken lines for all channels
  • Bad Pattern: Gaps, missing lines, or irregular patterns
  • White Channels: Need daily checks due to pigment settling
  • Color Channels: CMYK should show consistent coverage

2.2 Horizontal Banding

Appearance:

Visible horizontal lines or streaks running left-to-right across the print, in the direction of printhead carriage movement.

Visual Reference: Horizontal banding appears as consistent lines running left-to-right across the print.

For examples, see the OmniDTF Banding Solutions guide

Common Causes:

  • Clogged or deflected nozzles
  • Inconsistent ink flow
  • Low humidity environments (<40%)
  • Damaged printheads

Visual Characteristics:

  • Lines appear as "many very small lines, which cluster together"
  • Most visible in solid color areas
  • Particularly problematic with red/magenta colors

2.3 Vertical Banding

Appearance:

Vertical lines running perpendicular to printhead movement, often appearing as "flashing lines similar to test strip."

Visual Reference: Vertical banding appears as lines running perpendicular to printhead movement.

These lines often look like "flashing lines similar to test strips" and are typically caused by:

  • Dirty or damaged encoder strip
  • Encoder sensor misalignment
  • Film feed irregularities

For visual examples, see the Audley I3200 forum discussion

Common Causes:

  • Encoder strip damage or contamination
  • Sensor height adjustment issues
  • Media advancement problems
  • Mechanical alignment issues
Audley-Specific Issue: Registration wheel dust accumulation is a common cause of vertical banding and stretched prints on Audley printers.

2.4 Color-Specific Banding

White Ink Banding (Critical for 2W Configuration):

Visual Indicators of White Ink and Color-Specific Banding:

White Ink Banding Signs:
  • Gray or faded appearance instead of solid white
  • Horizontal lines with missing white coverage
  • Uneven opacity across the print
  • White ink completely missing in some areas
Color-Specific Dropout Signs:
  • Magenta/red channels showing gaps
  • Cyan dropping out intermittently
  • Individual color channels missing
  • Colors appearing in wrong channels

For real examples of these issues, visit DIY DTF Forums

  • Appearance: Gray appearance or complete white ink dropout
  • Cause: White ink pigment settling (can occur in 15 minutes)
  • Challenge: Titanium dioxide particles form gel-like substances
  • 2W Advantage: With 2 white heads, you have redundancy but double the maintenance

Color Channel Dropout:

  • Appearance: Missing ink in specific color channels
  • Cause: Individual nozzle blockages or damper issues
  • Cross-Contamination: Colors appearing in wrong channels

2.4 Print Head Alignment Problems

Misregistration Banding:

  • Colors not aligning properly between printhead passes
  • Different quality in left-right vs right-left printing directions
  • Visible in bidirectional printing test patterns

Comparison: Banding Issues vs. Good Print Quality

Horizontal Banding on Red Prints:
  • Visible horizontal lines across the print
  • Particularly noticeable on solid red areas
  • Lines appear as "clusters of small lines"
  • Most visible on shirts and solid colors
Good Nozzle Check Pattern Shows:
  • Complete, unbroken lines for all colors
  • CMYK channels clearly separated
  • No gaps or missing segments
  • At least 90% of nozzles functioning

4. Primary Causes of Banding

4.1 Ink Flow Problems (40% of cases)

Low Ink Levels:

  • Often overlooked fundamental cause of color dropouts
  • Must maintain ink above one-third capacity
  • Critical for consistent ink flow to printheads

Clogged Nozzles:

  • Dried ink blocking individual nozzles
  • Ink mist building up during longer print jobs
  • Creates gaps in coverage leading to banding

Air Bubbles in Ink Lines:

  • Disrupts consistent ink flow to printheads
  • Causes intermittent ink delivery
  • Requires "Load Ink" function to prime out

4.2 Printhead Issues (35% of cases)

Nozzle Problems:

  • Complete Blockage: Missing nozzles create light horizontal lines
  • Partial Blockage: Deflected nozzles cause ink to land incorrectly
  • Printhead Wear: Physical damage affects ink deposition patterns

Alignment Issues:

  • Misaligned printheads cause color registration problems
  • Bidirectional printing quality differences
  • Vertical distance misalignment between color and white printheads

4.3 Environmental Factors (15% of cases)

Critical Environmental Requirements:

  • Humidity: Must maintain 40-60% relative humidity
  • Temperature: Optimal range 68-77°F (20-25°C)
  • Air Quality: Dust-free environment to prevent contamination
  • Stability: Avoid temperature and humidity fluctuations

4.4 White Ink Specific Issues

Pigment Settling:

  • Titanium dioxide settles due to gravity in 15 minutes
  • Forms gel-like substances that clog nozzles and dampers
  • Requires continuous circulation and daily agitation

Maintenance Requirements:

  • Daily white ink cartridge agitation
  • Continuous circulation systems during operation
  • Annual ink bottle cleaning to remove sediment

5. Diagnostic Procedures

5.1 Initial Diagnosis - Nozzle Check

Step 1: Print Nozzle Check Pattern

Always start troubleshooting with this step

  1. Access nozzle check function in your printer software
  2. Print the test pattern
  3. Examine for missing lines, gaps, or inconsistent coverage
  4. Look for specific colors that are missing or printing poorly

Expected Results: A good nozzle check should show clear, unbroken lines for all colors with at least 90% of nozzles functioning properly.

✓ Good Nozzle Check Pattern

A perfect nozzle check should show:

  • Complete horizontal lines with no gaps
  • All CMYK colors clearly visible
  • Consistent line thickness
  • No deflection or misdirection
  • At least 90% nozzle functionality

Run this test daily before printing

✗ Bad Nozzle Check Pattern

Problems to look for:

  • Missing segments or gaps in lines
  • Entire color channels missing
  • Wavy or deflected lines
  • Intermittent firing patterns
  • Less than 90% nozzle function

Requires immediate cleaning

For detailed nozzle check analysis, see DCS Troubleshooting Guide (PDF)

5.2 Environmental Assessment

Check These Environmental Factors:

  • Measure current humidity level (should be 40-60%)
  • Check ambient temperature (should be 68-77°F)
  • Assess air circulation and drafts
  • Examine workspace for dust and contaminants
  • Verify printer has been at stable conditions for 30+ minutes

    • 6. Step-by-Step Solutions

    6.1 Basic Printhead Cleaning

    When to Perform:

    After every 100 prints or when banding appears. For Audley printers, clean every 2-3 days minimum.

    Printhead Cleaning Visual Guide:

    Proper printhead cleaning requires careful technique:

    1. Use lint-free cloths - Never use paper towels or tissues
    2. Dampen, don't soak - Excess liquid can damage electronics
    3. Gentle dabbing motion - Never wipe across nozzle plate
    4. Clean from outside to center - Prevents pushing debris into nozzles
    5. Allow proper drying time - 15-20 minutes before printing

    Video tutorials available at Procolored Maintenance Guide

    Materials Needed:

    • Lint-free cloths or microfiber cloths
    • DTF printhead cleaning solution or 90% isopropyl alcohol
    • Protective gloves
    • Cotton/foam swabs

    Procedure:

    1. Power down printer and unplug from power source
    2. Access printhead by moving it to maintenance position
    3. Remove ink cartridges and secure in plastic bags
    4. Exterior cleaning: Dampen lint-free cloth with cleaning solution
    5. Gentle wiping: Clean around printhead exterior to remove ink buildup
    6. Nozzle plate cleaning: Use cotton swabs dampened with cleaning solution to gently dab nozzle plate
    7. Automated cleaning cycle: Run printer's built-in cleaning function
    8. Reinstall cartridges and run another nozzle check

    6.2 Deep Cleaning for Persistent Clogs

    When to Perform:

    When basic cleaning doesn't resolve issues

    Additional Materials:

    • Small syringe (without needle)
    • Silicone tubing
    • Specialized DTF cleaning solution

    Advanced Procedure:

    1. Soak method: Place lint-free cloth soaked in cleaning solution on printhead for 10-15 minutes
    2. Syringe cleaning: Gently inject cleaning solution through nozzle plate using syringe
    3. Target specific areas: Focus on colors showing the most problems
    4. Allow drying time: Let printhead dry completely for 15-20 minutes before reinstalling

    6.3 White Ink System Maintenance (Critical for 2W Heads)

    Daily White Ink Care for Audley 2W Configuration:

    White Ink Circulation System Components:

    Critical for Audley 2W+2C configuration with dual white heads:

    • Circulation Pump: Runs continuously during operation
    • Filter System: Prevents titanium dioxide clumps
    • Tubing Network: Keeps white ink moving through both heads
    • Agitation Motor: Prevents pigment settling in bottles
    • Temperature Control: Maintains optimal ink viscosity

    Maintenance Tip: Check circulation system daily - white ink can settle in just 15 minutes!

    Learn more about white ink systems at Amaya UK Maintenance Tips

    1. Agitation: Shake white ink bottles thoroughly (2-3 minutes)
    2. Circulation: Run white ink circulation system continuously
    3. Monitoring: Check both white heads for separation or settling
    4. Testing: Print white ink test pattern to verify both heads

    Weekly White Ink Maintenance:

    1. System Prime: Use "Load Ink" function to prime white ink system
    2. Filter Check: Inspect white ink filters for clogs
    3. Line Inspection: Check all white ink lines for separation
    4. Waste Management: Empty waste ink bottle if needed

    6.4 Environmental Control Solutions

    Humidity Control (Critical for Audley Printers):

    🌡️ Environmental Monitoring Requirements:

    Critical for Audley I3200 Printers: Maintain 40-60% relative humidity

    Humidity Level Effect on Printing Action Required
    < 30% Severe nozzle clogging Add humidifier immediately
    30-40% Increased maintenance needed Monitor closely, add moisture
    40-60% Optimal performance Maintain current conditions
    > 60% Ink bleeding risk Use dehumidifier

    Equipment Needed: Digital hygrometer with min/max memory function

    • Target Range: Maintain 50% or higher (critical for Audley)
    • Monitoring: Use digital hygrometer for continuous tracking
    • Adjustment: Use humidifier/dehumidifier as needed
    • Consistency: Avoid rapid humidity changes

    Temperature Management:

    • Optimal Range: 68-77°F (20-25°C)
    • Stability: Minimize temperature fluctuations
    • Air Circulation: Ensure adequate ventilation without direct drafts
    • Equipment Warm-up: Allow 30 minutes for temperature stabilization

    6.5 Capping Station Maintenance

    Essential for Audley Printers:

    Capping Station Maintenance Procedure:

    The capping station seals printheads when idle - keeping it clean is critical:

    Required Tools:
    • 10ml syringe (no needle)
    • DTF cleaning solution
    • Lint-free cleaning pads
    • Cotton swabs
    • Small container for waste ink

    Key Steps:

    1. Extract accumulated ink with syringe
    2. Apply cleaning solution to dissolve dried ink
    3. Gently clean rubber cap edges
    4. Check for cracks or damage in rubber seals
    5. Test suction by performing a cleaning cycle

    For detailed instructions, see iColor DTF Maintenance Guide (PDF)

    Procedure:

    1. Use syringe to extract excess ink from capping station
    2. Apply cleaning solution to dissolve dried ink
    3. Wipe cap tops with lint-free cloth
    4. Check seal integrity - replace if damaged

    6.6 Encoder Strip Cleaning (Vertical Banding Fix)

    Critical for Audley Registration Issues:

    Encoder Strip Cleaning Guide:

    Critical Component: The encoder strip tells the printer where the printhead is positioned

    What to Look For:
    • Clear plastic strip with fine markings
    • Runs behind the printhead carriage
    • May have ink mist or dust buildup
    • Check for scratches or damage
    Cleaning Process:
    • Use 90% isopropyl alcohol only
    • Wipe gently with lint-free cloth
    • Clean entire length of strip
    • Allow to dry completely

    Warning: Never use acetone or harsh solvents - they will damage the strip!

    This fixes vertical banding and "duplicated lines" issues common on Audley printers

    Procedure:

    1. Locate encoder strip (clear plastic strip with markings)
    2. Dampen lint-free cloth with isopropyl alcohol
    3. Gently wipe entire length of strip
    4. Check for damage or scratches
    5. Clean registration wheel to prevent stretched prints

    7. Preventive Maintenance Schedule

    Daily Maintenance (5-10 minutes)

    • Turn on white ink circulation system
    • Perform nozzle check before printing
    • Clean around printhead exterior
    • Wipe down wiper blades
    • Check ink levels
    • Empty waste ink bottle if needed
    • Agitate white ink bottles (2-3 minutes)

    Weekly Maintenance (30-45 minutes)

    • Deep clean printhead if needed
    • Clean capping station cap tops
    • Flush capping station with cleaning solution
    • Clean pinch rollers thoroughly
    • Clean encoder strip with isopropyl alcohol
    • Check for air bubbles in ink lines
    • Inspect white ink system for separation

    Monthly Maintenance (1-2 hours)

    • Power ink flush if severe nozzle issues
    • Grease carriage rails if squeaking
    • Clean encoder disk
    • Comprehensive system calibration
    • Check all mechanical components
    • Replace ink filters
    • Clean white ink bottles interior

    Quarterly Maintenance (2-3 hours)

    • Replace ink cartridges
    • Replace ink pads
    • Replace white ink filters
    • Deep clean white ink bottles
    • Professional calibration check
    • Damper replacement assessment
    • Complete system performance review

    Extended Downtime Preparation:

    If your printer will be idle for more than 3 weeks, contact technical support to schedule an appointment and prepare the printer for extended downtime or storage. Failure to do so can result in permanent printhead damage.

    Damper Replacement Guide:

    Critical for Audley Printers: Dampers are consumable parts that filter ink and regulate flow

    When to Replace Dampers:
    • Every 6-12 months as preventive maintenance
    • When persistent clogs don't clear with cleaning
    • If you see air bubbles that won't prime out
    • When ink flow becomes inconsistent
    • After any major ink contamination event

    Audley-Specific Tips:

    • Keep spare dampers on hand - critical for 4-head systems
    • Replace all dampers at once for consistent performance
    • Use 2mm tubes instead of 3mm for better siphon action
    • Mark replacement date on each damper

    Dampers typically cost $10-20 each - much cheaper than printhead replacement!

    10.1 Film Feed Calibration (Audley-Specific)

    Fixing Stretched Prints and Registration Issues:

    Film Feed Calibration - Audley Specific Issue:

    Common Problem: Prints appear stretched or show duplicated lines

    Registration Wheel Issues:
    • Dust accumulation on wheel
    • Film slipping during feed
    • Incorrect pressure settings
    • Worn rubber surface
    Cleaning Process:
    • Clean wheel with alcohol weekly
    • Check for smooth rotation
    • Adjust pressure if needed
    • Replace if worn

    Calibration Steps:

    1. Print calibration pattern
    2. Measure actual vs expected length
    3. Adjust feed calibration value in software
    4. Test with another print

    See discussion at Audley Duplicated Lines Forum Thread

    • Common Audley Issue: Designs printing stretched or duplicated lines
    • Cause: Dust on registration wheel or incorrect feed calibration
    • Solution: Clean registration wheel weekly, recalibrate film feed
    • Prevention: Keep printer covered when not in use
